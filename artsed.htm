About Lauren Jost

 

I started my career as a Teaching Artist fifteen years ago as an apprentice at the Seattle Children's Theater.  I was very interested in non-scripted theatre. The apprenticeship included devising youth theatre and creative process drama.  Many of the creative drama classes were pre-kindergarten groups, and I quickly fell in love with the quirky imaginations and immediate belief of this unique age group.  Since then I have taught babies, high schoolers, senior citizens, and every age in between, but my heart has always been at home in the early childhood classroom. 

 

For the last ten years I have been a Teaching Artist at the New Victory Theater, which brings performers from all over the world to New York City family audiences.  I soon found that the most exciting, innovative, and artistically inspiring performances were the ones made for ages 0-5. Most of these companies were from Europe, and I knew I wanted to create this kind of work myself. No one in New York was making it.  

 

Several years ago I started a family and was spending less time teaching and more time with my own children.  This shift gave me time to pursue my own creative dreams, and I founded Spellbound Theatre and created our first performance, Wink, for ages 0-5. The company has grown quickly, and while we are still one of only a few companies in the United States making theatre exclusively for ages 0-5, we have found an eager audience of families excited about arts programs designed with young children at the center. It is always challenging, working as Teaching Artist, director, sometimes performer, and always producer of a young company. I find the work exciting, fulfilling, and creatively challenging. 

 

About Spellbound Theater

 

Spellbound Theatre is a new company devoted to creating original, multi-disciplinary performance for the very young.  Spellbound produces original plays for audiences ages 0-5 and conducts early childhood education programs and community events for young children.  We believe that young people deserve sophisticated and innovative art experiences and are constantly inspired by our complex and imaginative world. 

Founded in 2011, Spellbound Theatre has performed to sold-out family audiences at numerous venues in Brooklyn and Manhattan, including BAX, The Old Stone House, and the City College of New York.  Spellbound Theatre is a not-for-profit organization, and has received grant funding for their innovative work in theatre for young audiences from The Jim Henson Foundation, The NYC Department of Cultural Affairs (administered by the Brooklyn Arts Council), and the New York State Council of the Arts (administered by Arts Westchester), the Park Slope Civic Council, and has received support from the Brooklyn Arts Council and Materials for the Arts (NYC DCA). 


This year, Spellbound Theatre is producing a full season of 4 original shows and is a New Victory Theatre LabWorks Resident Artist, working on a new play for young audiences that will be presented at the New 42nd Street Studios.
