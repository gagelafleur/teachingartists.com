Sojn Boothroyd 

srb980@mail.harvard.edu

 		  
   

Sojn Boothroyd is an educator, social justice activist, and award-winning interdisciplinary artist with twenty years of experience working with K-12 youth in schools and community centers throughout Washington State, Chicago, Boston, New York City, and Kalamazoo, Michigan. Sojn Boothroyd is a published author, holds an Ed.M. in Arts in Education from the Harvard Graduate School of Education, and was one of twelve master Teaching Artists featured on a national online project by the Association of Teaching Artists in 2013. Sojn’s teaching and organizational leadership experience includes training educators across the U.S. and working for three organizations that have received the National Arts and Humanities Youth Program Award for outstanding after-school and out-of-school programs: Seattle’s Arts Corps, Street-Level Youth Media, and Marwen in Chicago.

Sojn was also the director and Teaching Artist for a youth-led PhotoVoice project on the relationship between sexual health and racism—developed by youth at the Douglass Community Association in Kalamazoo. The project was presented at the Kalamazoo County Health Equity Summit, and then brought to an international public health conference in South Africa in September 2013. Currently, Sojn conducts ongoing residency projects with youth, educators, and activists, and is a faculty member at Northeastern University and Western Michigan University. Sojn is also the creator of an arts-integration project that takes an intersectional approach to address bullying and bias-based harassment such as racism, transphobia, and homophobia in K-12 schools.
